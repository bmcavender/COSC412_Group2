Soccer Information

Health for Soccer

Eat a moderate amount of fat-rich foods for an energy reserve to maintain your endurance for long soccer matches.

Healthy fats like salmon, avocados, and nuts are great sources of fat
for the soccer athlete. They also have many anti-inflammatory benefits and
aid in recovery. Focus on eating fats prior to your games. A lot of
carbohydrates are also necessary for a good diet for soccer playing in order
to provide energy to get through practice and games. Slow-digesting carbs
like apples and cherries are the best option to spread that energy throughout
the game so that you don't top off too early. Lastly, protein is a necessity
for maintaining and recovering muscles after all that hard work. Lean proteins
are best to ensure isolation of that particular nutrient. "The less legs the better!"
When recovering after playing a game or working out, soccer athletes should eat mostly proteins and some carbs. A good example
could be a healthy burrito with chicken, avocado, veggies, black beans and cheese.

Fitness for Soccer

Loosen up your hips with leg swings! Also try these calf stretches to cool down after playing.

Training for Soccer

Dribble the soccer ball in circles around the cones to improve your dribbling and coordination. Single leg line jumps are great for explosiveness, balance, and agility.
To perform, just stand on one leg and hop from side to side over a line. Interval sprints is great for increasing your endurance for soccer.
Try sprinting down the sides when doing laps around the soccer field.
